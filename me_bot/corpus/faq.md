# Frequently asked questions

## Background and education

### Tell me about Raven's background.

Raven Roberts is a first-generation scientist from Appalachia, based
in Newport, Tennessee. She started building computers and writing
code at age eight, began dual enrollment at thirteen, and has
maintained 4.0 GPAs at both Harvard Extension School and Roane State
Community College alongside three years of production AI engineering
experience at Crayon (remote US and Vienna) and Flomoney (Singapore).
She founded Tardigrade Innovation LLC in fall 2024 and is matriculating
at Louisiana State University as a B.S. Physics student in fall 2026.

### Where did Raven study?

Raven completed associate degrees at St. Petersburg College (3.89 GPA,
2018–2024) starting at age thirteen as a dual-enrollment student. She
then enrolled concurrently at Roane State Community College and
Harvard Extension School, maintaining a 4.0 GPA at both. She is
matriculating at Louisiana State University in fall 2026.

### What is Raven studying at LSU?

Physics, B.S. She received the Academic Scholars Nonresident Award —
a competitive merit scholarship — and chose LSU specifically for its
gravitational-wave faculty and its proximity to LIGO Livingston.

### What languages does Raven speak?

English (native), German (fluent), and Russian (fluent). The German
came from her summer in Vienna with Crayon in 2023.

### Where is Raven based?

Newport, Tennessee, in East Tennessee — the eastern Appalachian region
of the state. She is moving to Baton Rouge for the LSU program in
summer 2026.

## Current work

### What is Tardigrade Innovation?

Tardigrade Innovation LLC is an independent research firm Raven
founded in 2024. It operates Tardigrade Quantum Laboratory in
Newport, Tennessee, and serves as the institutional home for her
hardware builds, software products, patent applications, and
collaborative research.

### What is Raven working on right now?

The most active research thread is a variational quantum eigensolver
classifier for LIGO gravitational-wave signals, in collaboration with
a UCSB PhD candidate and run on real IBM Quantum hardware (ibm_nairobi).
The arXiv preprint is in preparation as of 2026. Alongside the
classifier work, the lab in Newport has seven active hardware rigs in
various states of operation — including an MPCVD reactor for NV-center
diamond, a Paul trap toward Yb⁺ ion trapping, and a 4-meter L-band
radio interferometer.

### What's Raven's research focus?

Her research lives at the intersection of quantum computation,
gravitational-wave astronomy, and physics-constrained embodied AI.
The interesting physics, in her framing, happens at that seam.

### Is Raven looking for collaborators?

Yes — actively, especially around the VQE/LIGO classifier work, the
Clifford geometric algebra agent, and the L-band interferometer's
pulsar timing program. The contact form on the site is the right
route for collaboration inquiries.

### Does Raven supervise students?

Not formally — she is herself an undergraduate. She does correspond
with other early-career researchers and first-generation scientists
who write to her, and is consistent about answering those messages.

### Is Raven taking on consulting clients?

She takes on a limited number of consulting engagements per year
through Tardigrade Innovation LLC, typically in quantum machine
learning, gravitational-wave data pipelines, hardware design, or
graduate application strategy. The contact form is the right way to
inquire.

## The Appalachian mission

### What's the connection to Appalachia?

Raven is from East Tennessee and intends to come back. The longer arc
of her career is to build a multi-modal research institution in
Appalachia — combining radio astronomy, gravitational-wave
instrumentation, and quantum materials science, anchored to the land
by intention rather than federal committee. The model in mind is
something closer to Green Bank, West Virginia: a serious research
site whose presence shapes regional land use and provides skilled
local employment.

### Why does this matter?

A serious research institution in East Tennessee creates jobs that
can be learned locally and don't require leaving the region, and it
provides a structural protection against the development pressure
that's been hollowing out Appalachia for a generation. Raven is open
about the political dimension of this.

## Contact

### How can I contact Raven?

The contact form on the site is the best route. She reads every
message and replies inside 48 hours, usually faster. For students and
early-career researchers, especially folks from rural and
first-generation backgrounds — yes, you can write cold.

### How do I write a good cold email to Raven?

Be specific about what you're working on, why your work overlaps
with hers, and what you're actually asking for. One paragraph is
better than three.
