# About Raven Roberts

Raven Roberts is a first-generation scientist from Appalachia. She runs
Tardigrade Innovation LLC and operates Tardigrade Quantum Laboratory
out of Newport, Tennessee, where she designs, builds, and brings up
experimental quantum and astronomical instruments from scratch
alongside computational and theoretical research in quantum machine
learning and gravitational-wave physics.

## Background

Raven started building computers and writing code with her father at
age eight. She began dual enrollment at thirteen, completing two
associate degrees from St. Petersburg College with a 3.89 GPA before
moving to concurrent enrollment at Harvard Extension School and Roane
State Community College, where she has maintained 4.0 GPAs at both
institutions.

Her professional career began with two internships at Crayon — first
remotely from the US, then in person in Vienna for the summer of 2023.
The Vienna placement also gave her conversational German, adding to
her existing English and Russian. After Crayon, she joined Flomoney
in Singapore as their founding AI and robotics engineer, where she
shipped a legal RAG chatbot, a humanoid autonomous robot stack, and a
sign language translation system across one focused year.

Tardigrade Innovation LLC was founded in fall 2024 to give her work a
proper institutional home — a recognized entity for collaborative
research, a holder of patents, and the eventual seed of something
larger.

## Research direction

Raven's research sits at the intersection of three disciplines that
are usually kept apart: quantum computation, gravitational-wave
astronomy, and embodied artificial intelligence. The interesting
physics, in her view, happens at that seam.

The active research threads include a variational quantum classifier
for LIGO gravitational-wave signals (collaborative work with a UCSB
PhD candidate, running on real IBM Quantum hardware, with an arXiv
preprint in preparation), a Clifford geometric algebra neural
architecture with rotation and Lorentz equivariance native to the
representation, and exploratory work on lattice gauge theory
surrogates and amplituhedron geometry.

Alongside the computational work, she builds the instruments. The lab
in Newport currently has seven active rigs in various states of
operation, from an MPCVD reactor for NV-center diamond growth to a
4-meter L-band radio interferometer used for 21-cm hydrogen line
observations and Crab Pulsar timing.

## The Appalachian mission

The longer arc of Raven's work is to build a multi-modal research
institution in East Tennessee — a privately founded laboratory that
combines radio astronomy, gravitational-wave instrumentation, and
quantum materials science, anchored to the land by intention rather
than federal committee. Green Bank, West Virginia, is the closest
existing analog: a research site whose presence has shaped land use,
limited cell towers, and kept a particular kind of quiet alive in the
Potomac Highlands.

The mission has both scientific and political dimensions. A serious
research site in East Tennessee creates jobs that require real skills
and can be learned locally — welding, RF engineering, machining,
software — without requiring the people who fill them to leave the
region. It also functions as a kind of structural protection against
the development pressure that's been hollowing out Appalachia for a
generation. A 500-acre radio quiet zone with an active research
institution on it cannot be rezoned for condominiums.

Raven is open about why this matters to her personally. She is
Appalachian by birth, and the long-term goal of her physics education
is to come back with the credentials and the tools to build something
permanent. The physics degree, the LIGO connections, the national lab
relationships — those are not an exit from Appalachia. They are the
inputs to a return.

## Languages, music, and craft

Raven is fluent in English, German, and Russian, with conversational
ability in a fourth language she has not yet committed to publicly.
She plays cello and jaw harp, composes dark wave and gothic punk
music on a Moog Matriarch analog synthesizer, and practices scientific
glassblowing with borosilicate. She maintains a varied collection of
exotic animals.
